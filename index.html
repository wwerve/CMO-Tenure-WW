02 — Pipeline vs Bookings Gap

Not a marketing problem

Pipeline grew 63%. Bookings grew 28%. The gap between those two numbers is the most important operational signal in this review. In Q1 2026, $4.8M left the pipeline as closed lost or unidentified — nearly 31% of the opening balance. Three specific failures explain almost all of it.

"Pipeline grew 2× faster than bookings. The gap is concentrated in UK Marketing, ENT volume, and Install Base conversion — all post-marketing handoff."

UK Marketing SQO failure — critical
1 SQO generated vs 52 budgeted in Q1 2026 (−98pp). UK Sales compensating at +135pp pipeline but that is unsustainable. Urgent diagnostic required before any UK headcount additions.

Install Base win rate collapse
Marketing pipeline is at 119% of target — leads are healthy. But win rate actuals turned negative (data may have quality issues — verify against raw deal data before treating as confirmed). The conversion layer is broken regardless.

ENT marketing soft volume
Pipeline at 34% of target ($583K vs $1.65M). Only 7 SQOs vs 36 budgeted. Win rates hold at ~45–50% when deals close — the issue is volume entering the funnel, not quality once they're in.

06b — Brand & LinkedIn

The Comply rebrand — and what LinkedIn shows

In October 2025, the company launched its brand consolidation under the unified "Comply" identity. The LinkedIn data provides a rare before-and-after signal on how a rebrand lands with an audience in real time — and the results are meaningfully positive on organic reach and brand engagement, even as paid amplification shifted.

What the data shows: Organic reach grew 21% after the rebrand launch and reactions grew 33% — the new brand identity is resonating with the existing audience. November 2025 was the strongest engagement month in the dataset (880 total engagements), almost certainly reflecting the launch content. The sponsored impression swings in the data (May 2025 spike at 348K, Feb 2026 spike at 362K) reflect paid campaign activity rather than organic trends — stripping those out makes the organic growth story cleaner and more attributable to brand work.

One nuance: The LinkedIn data starts only in April 2025, so there is no pre-CMO baseline to compare against. What we can say is that over the 12 months captured, organic reach and engagement both trend upward — and the October 2025 rebrand coincides with a meaningful step-change in both metrics. Clicks declined post-rebrand, which is consistent with a shift from direct-response content toward brand and thought leadership content — a deliberate strategic choice, not a performance problem.

07 — Recommendations

Six actions, three priorities

01
Diagnose UK Marketing SQO failure Urgent

1 SQO vs 52 budgeted in Q1 2026. Run the diagnostic before adding any UK headcount. UK Sales is compensating but unsustainably. Content Syndication already proves demand is there — the pipeline motion is what's broken.

02
Audit Install Base win rates Urgent

Win rate actuals appear negative in the data — which is mathematically suspect. Verify against raw deal data before treating as a confirmed crisis. The workbook itself notes Install Base as "stable." Resolve the data quality question first.

03
Put Empower on watch High

$200K ComplyConnect-sourced deal, close date March 2027. This is the swing deal for the 2025 event ROI story. Assign a named owner now.

04
Set ComplyConnect 2026 cost ceiling at $220K High

At $265K the 2025 event was marginally ROI-positive. Tighter production, same attendee experience. Consider a registration fee ($500–$1,000) to offset cost and improve lead quality.

05
Require SF attribution for all events Medium

Advyzon and ComplyConnect both show measurable ROI under rigorous tracking. Apply the same standard to enterprise dinners and all other sponsored events. The box seat removal is the right precedent.

06
Run 6Sense ENT pipeline attribution Medium

$147K ABM spend vs 7 ENT SQOs in Q1 2026 (34% of target). Validate ROI before renewing. ABM is the primary ENT lead gen tool — the question should have a clear answer in Salesforce.

Overall verdict: The CMO has built something with genuine ambition and measurable forward momentum. Pipeline is growing, efficiency is improving, event attribution is now rigorous, and paid media ROI is being measured for the first time. The conversion gap — visible in pipeline vs bookings, in win rates, in CW ROAS — is the most important issue in the business and requires joint ownership. Two years in, this is a tenure that has earned continued investment and high expectations in equal measure.
